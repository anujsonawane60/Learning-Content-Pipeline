Module 1: Introduction to AI

This module provides a broad overview of artificial intelligence and its real-world applications.

Chapter 1: What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think and learn. These systems can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, and decision-making.

AI has evolved significantly over the past decade. Modern AI systems use deep learning and neural networks to process vast amounts of data and identify patterns that would be impossible for humans to detect manually.

The field of AI encompasses several subfields including machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and robotics.

Chapter 2: History and Evolution of AI

The concept of AI dates back to the 1950s when Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test as a measure of machine intelligence. Early AI research focused on symbolic reasoning and problem-solving.

In the 1980s and 1990s, machine learning gained traction as researchers developed algorithms that could learn from data rather than relying on explicit programming rules.

The deep learning revolution began around 2012, when neural networks started outperforming traditional methods on image recognition tasks. This breakthrough was enabled by larger datasets and more powerful hardware.

Chapter 3: Types of AI Systems

AI systems can be categorized into three types:

Narrow AI (Weak AI) is designed for a specific task. Examples include virtual assistants, recommendation systems, and image classifiers. This is the only type of AI that exists today.

General AI (Strong AI) would have human-level intelligence across all domains. This remains a theoretical concept and has not yet been achieved.

Superintelligent AI would surpass human intelligence in every aspect. This is a topic of ongoing debate among researchers and ethicists.

Module 2: Prompt Engineering Basics

Learn how to communicate effectively with large language models through well-crafted prompts.

Chapter 1: Understanding Prompts

A prompt is the input text you provide to an AI language model. The quality and structure of your prompt directly affects the quality of the response you receive.

Good prompts are clear, specific, and provide sufficient context. They tell the AI exactly what you need and in what format you want the response.

Think of prompting as giving instructions to a very capable but literal assistant. The more precise your instructions, the better the output.

Chapter 2: Prompt Techniques

There are several proven techniques for writing effective prompts:

Zero-shot prompting asks the model to perform a task without any examples. This works well for simple, well-defined tasks.

Few-shot prompting provides a few examples of the desired input-output pairs before asking the model to complete a new instance. This helps the model understand the pattern you expect.

Chain-of-thought prompting asks the model to show its reasoning step by step. This is especially useful for complex reasoning tasks like math problems or logical deductions.

Chapter 3: Common Mistakes and Best Practices

Avoid vague or ambiguous prompts. Instead of asking "Tell me about dogs," specify what aspect you want to know about, such as "List the top 5 dog breeds for apartment living and explain why each is suitable."

Do not assume the AI has context from previous conversations unless you are in a continuous chat session. Always provide necessary background information.

Break complex tasks into smaller steps. Rather than asking the AI to write an entire business plan, ask for one section at a time.

Module 3: Hands-On AI Tools

Practical exercises using popular AI platforms and APIs.

Chapter 1: Getting Started with ChatGPT

ChatGPT is a conversational AI model developed by OpenAI. It can answer questions, write content, debug code, and assist with a wide variety of tasks.

To get started, create an account at the OpenAI platform. You can use the free tier for basic access or subscribe to a paid plan for advanced features like GPT-4 access.

Experiment with different prompts to understand how the model responds. Try asking it to explain concepts, generate creative content, or help with coding tasks.

Chapter 2: Using AI APIs

APIs (Application Programming Interfaces) allow you to integrate AI capabilities into your own applications programmatically.

The OpenAI API provides access to models like GPT-4 and DALL-E. You can send requests with your prompt and receive structured responses that your application can process.

To use the API, you need an API key. Keep your API key secure and never share it publicly. Set up environment variables to manage your keys safely.
